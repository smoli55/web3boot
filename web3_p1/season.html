Whats in season?

‹

Fall

›

Fall in Quebec offers a rich harvest of hearty and flavorful produce.These fall ingredients lend themselves well to warming soups, comforting casseroles, and all the cozy flavors of the season.
Apples: Numerous varieties for snacking, baking, and cider-making.

Pears: Sweet and juicy pears come into season in the fall.

Pumpkins: Ideal for carving, decorating, and making delicious pies.

Squash: Butternut, acorn, and spaghetti squash are fall staples.

Cranberries: Tart and vibrant, perfect for sauces and desserts.

Grapes: Both table grapes and those for making wine are harvested.

Plums: Sweet and versatile, great for both sweet and savory dishes.

Brussels sprouts: Small and mighty, these are a fall favorite.

Sweet potatoes: A nutritious and tasty fall root vegetable.

Carrots: Sweet and crisp, perfect for soups and roasting.

Beets: Earthy and colorful, great for salads and roasting.

Turnips: A versatile root vegetable with a mild flavor.

Cabbage: Red and green cabbage are abundant in the fall.

Kale: This leafy green is at its best in the cooler fall months.

Leeks: Mild and onion-like, perfect for soups and stews.
